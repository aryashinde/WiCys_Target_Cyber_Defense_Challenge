Target Cyber Defense Challenge


Currently, this write-up covers my experience and solutions for the Target Cyber Defense Challenge, which was organized through WiCys from July 1, 2025, to August 14, 2025. I participated in this challenge and would like to share my understanding, approaches, and insights gained while solving the various cybersecurity tasks throughout the event.

Tier One

Scenario:

The team at Personalyz.io was thrown into crisis mode when alerts flashed across their security dashboard. Overnight, a ransomware attack had struck. Critical files were encrypted, and sensitive data had been exfiltrated.
A shadowy figure, known only as Shadow Gopher, had already infiltrated the company’s networks. Every action taken by the incident response team, every log analyzed, every system inspected, was critical to contain the breach and limit the damage.
With the clock ticking, Personalyz.io had to investigate the incident, trace the attacker’s steps, and respond effectively to protect its assets and its highly anticipated IPO.

Conclusion:

Based on solving the challenges, it became clear that the data breach was orchestrated through a combination of hardware and software manipulation. The attacker used a TinyPilot KVM device to gain persistent remote access, exploited API endpoints and system vulnerabilities, and manipulated internal systems to exfiltrate sensitive data. The investigation revealed how small oversights, like exposed APIs and misconfigured systems, allowed the attacker to move through the environment and obtain critical information. Ultimately, the challenges demonstrated the importance of layered security and careful monitoring to prevent insider threats and unauthorized access.

Tier Two

Scenario:

As part of Shadow Gopher’s crew, I navigated Personalyz.io’s network, executing key operations while also handling smaller side-task challenges. Each step, whether exfiltrating data, probing systems, or testing defenses, was part of a larger mission. The company scrambled to respond, but every alert I triggered helped me understand their environment better, letting me move efficiently and maintain control over the breach.

Conclusion:

Throughout these challenges, I operated from a red team perspective, gaining access to Personalyz.io through crafted resumes and internal exploitation. I identified and leveraged vulnerabilities across web apps, LDAP directories, SMB shares, and encrypted archives, escalating privileges and extracting sensitive data. Side challenges tested my skills in reverse engineering, template exploitation, and code execution. Overall, the exercises demonstrated my ability to systematically explore environments, uncover weaknesses, and exfiltrate valuable information.
